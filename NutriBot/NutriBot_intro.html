Introduction: Why NutriBot

Imagine you walk into a nutritionist's office and hand them a thick binder of your medical history, allergies, and dietary preferences. Now imagine they read it instantly and give you tailored advice in seconds.

That’s what we wanted NutriBot to feel like.

Most AI chatbots today are fast talkers—but poor listeners. They ignore context, miss nuance, and offer generic advice. We wanted to flip that. NutriBot was our team’s attempt at building a context-aware AI assistant—one that could understand documents, recall relevant information, and reason like an expert using modern GenAI tooling.

It started with a simple question: Can we design an AI assistant that reads like a clinician and responds like a dietitian?

The project was a collaborative effort:

  • I focused on designing and engineering the backend architecture, including the Agent RAG system, prompt orchestration, and AWS Bedrock deployment of foundation models.

  • One teammate led the React-based frontend, giving users an intuitive way to interact with the assistant.

  • Another tackled food image classification, building a computer vision module to extract nutritional details from plate images.

As a backend and GenAI engineer, my focus was on making the assistant smart—by integrating:

  • LLM Agents with tool-based reasoning using LangChain

  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for grounded answers from PDFs and documents

  • Prompt engineering techniques to improve accuracy and personalization

  • A scalable LLM deployment layer using AWS Bedrock

  • High-performance vector search via Chroma for fast, in-memory vector search over chunked healthcare documents.

NutriBot became our hands-on lab for understanding what it takes to build real-world GenAI systems—especially in healthcare, where answers must be not only fast, but factual. This blog is a walk-through of the architecture we built, the tools I learned, and the GenAI systems I now feel confident designing from the ground up.
